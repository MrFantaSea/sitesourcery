Day to day

How it works

How do I change my site?

On a care plan, you text me in plain English — “Add Saturday hours,” “Make the lemon tarts the big photo,” “Raise the haircut price to $30.” I make the change and reply when it’s live, usually the same day.

There’s no dashboard, no app, and nothing to learn. Text-to-change comes with a care plan (from $29/mo); the buy-once build is yours either way.

Who am I actually working with?

Site Sourcery, directly. A real person on a real number who knows your site and picks up when you reach out — the same point of contact every time.

Everything shown on this site is work I actually built.

I already have a website or domain. Can you use it?

Yes. I rebuild it properly and bring your domain across — I handle the move end to end. You keep your address; it just finally points at a site that earns it.

The Responder · pre-launch concept

A planned workflow for missed calls

What is The Responder?

It is a proposed automation workflow: after an unanswered call, an approved text would be sent where provider, carrier, consent, and legal requirements allow it. It is not operating as a public production service.

The displayed $99/mo figure is a planning estimate, not a current offer. Existing-number support, delivery timing, and availability depend on provider and carrier approval. See the requirements and pre-launch status.

Is it a bot pretending to be a human?

That is a launch requirement: an automated message must not impersonate a person. Any future configuration must use approved content, honor opt-outs, provide a human follow-up path, and be monitored for failures.

No production claim is made until those controls are implemented and tested.

Can I try it now?

Not yet. There is no public Responder demo or production onboarding. The published phone number is a normal contact line, not an automated trial.

A trial will be advertised only after carrier registration, provider setup, consent and opt-out tests, delivery/failure tests, monitoring, and security review pass.

Money & ownership

What’s yours, in writing

Do I own my site?

Yes. The design, the words, the photos, and your domain are yours. If you ever leave, I hand everything over and help you move it — no exit fee, no files held hostage.

I’d rather you stay because it’s working than because you’re stuck.

Can I cancel?

Website Care is described as month-to-month and cancellable by text or email. The Hive is not currently operating, so there is no Responder subscription to cancel. Any future Hive agreement must state cancellation, data export, retention, provider, and shutdown terms before activation. The website and domain remain yours after final payment.

What does it cost?

Custom websites are buy-once: eight tiers from $200 (Card) up to $3,000 (Flagship), and you own it outright — no subscription to rent your own site. Optional monthly care runs from $15/mo up to $159/mo and adds hosting, updates, text-to-change, and the monthly note.

The Responder and other Hive workflows are not currently for sale. Their $99/mo starting point and $219/mo bundle are planning estimates for feedback. An inquiry is non-binding; the pricing page keeps those estimates separate from current website pricing.

Being found

The honest part

Can you guarantee I show up first on Google?

No — and anyone who promises that is lying to you. Nobody controls Google, and no honest shop pretends to.

What I actually do: build your site so it’s easy to find — fast pages, clean structure, and your name, town, hours and services written where search can read them — so you’re the name their phone recommends when someone nearby asks for the best plumber or bakery. I never promise a #1 ranking; I build so the answer can be you.

Then the monthly note shows how it’s actually moving. Results vary; honesty doesn’t.

How do I know it’s working?

On the Care plan ($59/mo) and up, once a month you get a plain-English note — a sample month reads “11 calls, 5 jobs booked, 3 new reviews” — no charts to decode, no login to check. If a number dips, the note says so, and what I’m doing about it.

Real numbers only. The sample above is just an illustration — results vary, and I never guarantee them. See how the note works.
