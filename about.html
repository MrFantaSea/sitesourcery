Who does what

AI gets it built, fast. A real person makes sure it's good.

The basics are the part no one should have to think about: it loads fast on a phone, works for everyone, and is built so Google and AI can read it and hand people your name. The AI nails that every single time, without getting tired.

The rest is the part worth a person: that it looks right, sounds like you, and says nothing you can't stand behind. A computer can't care whether a claim is true. I can — so I check every one, on every site, before it's yours.

It's also why it costs what it costs. A custom site like this used to be a $5,000 job, because it took weeks of a person's time. AI does the slow part in a fraction of that. The site is just as custom; I passed the savings on to you.

One note on the tool: what matters is the work and the person behind it, not a brand name to drop. So I don't name the AI — I put my name on the result instead.
