Scope, quote & payment

If a project is commercially accepted, its scope is the authorized website footprint tier plus the signed Scope & Job Manifest prepared for that job. The manifest records the current-site inventory, delivered pages and URL map, capabilities, transition and migration work, content responsibilities, revision rounds, Care choice, outside services, target schedule, and total price. Public planning figures cannot substitute for that document.

  • A replacement quote starts with the current public footprint. Before we quote, we verify the existing public pages, locations, major collections, and tools. Each current URL must have a written keep, merge, redirect, or retire disposition. A concept or teaser page is not the quoted footprint.
  • Consolidation is a recommendation, not an automatic discount. A material reduction in pages or public paths requires an approved architecture plan and any applicable transition work. We will not collapse a larger site merely to force it into a cheaper tier.
  • If commercial authority later opens and a quote is issued, it is open for 14 calendar days. After that, price and availability must be reconfirmed before any job could be booked.
  • Commercial authorization comes before payment. No public page or inquiry accepts a catalog price. If a project is approved for intake, the written agreement states the required deposit or up-front payment and when work is authorized to begin.
  • The planning payment model is tier-aware. Card ($350) and Card Plus ($600) are modeled as paid up front; larger fixed tiers are modeled as 50% to begin and the balance on delivery. The authorized manifest controls any actual project.
  • Launch follows approval and final payment. You review the completed scope, approve it for launch in writing, pay the remaining balance, and then the site goes live or the finished files are handed over.
  • No unapproved extras. The signed price changes only through a written, paid change order or an agreed tier upgrade.

Footprint, capability & transition pricing

The non-binding footprint preview is catalog SS-TIERS-2026.3, and the non-binding addition preview is catalog SS-ADDONS-2026.3. The footprint band prices delivered public-page capacity and light editing of client-supplied facts/content. Site and larger fixed bands have capacity for at most one basic form only when that form is explicitly named in the written scope; no form is inferred from page count. Card and Card Plus may add one bounded basic form. Capped additions price additional content shaping, special design, motion, tools, providers, collections, connections, revisions, priority, architecture, or structured-data migration. An addition is never ordered by checking a box or sending an inquiry.

  • Only a commercially authorized, signed manifest can become an order. It must record both catalog IDs, the base footprint tier, each addition and quantity, unit prices, URL dispositions, content or record counts, prerequisites, provider dependencies or fees, and any schedule effect.
  • Caps keep custom work visible. Each addition remains subject to its published cap. Work past a cap, more than 15 delivered pages, multiple custom tools, a CMS, dashboard, backend, database, custom API, authentication, custom checkout, managed automation, or comparable complexity is Beyond scope. Unused capacity is not cash, credit, a swap, or a discount.
  • Capabilities do not disappear inside a page tier. Bespoke visuals, custom motion, standard tools, hosted-provider integrations, and structured collections are itemized even when the page footprint itself is small.
  • Page capacity is a ceiling, not the only sizing axis. A higher band may serve fewer public pages when its section, layout, content, media, form, or revision capacity is the honest fit.
  • Transition work is explicit. One or two ordinary redirects are included where appropriate. An approved architecture and redirect plan is modeled at $350 for 3–5 affected URLs, $650 for 6–10, and $950 for 11–15. More than 15, complex recovery or cleanup, email migration, or broad information architecture is Beyond scope.
  • Structured-data migration depends on clean source material. The first block of up to 100 clean, exportable entries in one approved schema is modeled at $350; each next block of up to 100 is $200 through 500 total entries. Manual reconstruction, inconsistent records, inaccessible systems, media remediation, several schemas, or more than 500 entries requires Beyond scope.
  • Connections remain client-owned. An additional connection covers the published domain or provider connection work. We request time-limited, least-privilege access when needed; do not send credentials or recovery codes through the inquiry form. Provider fees are separate.
  • Priority is conditional. A priority production window could exist only after commercial authority opens, capacity is confirmed, and the project is signed, paid, and ready. It changes queue position only — never QA, scope, third-party timing, or a guaranteed date or outcome.

Ownership & outside services

After final payment, Site Sourcery’s original custom deliverables made for your approved scope transfer to you. Your website is not rented from us, and you may take the paid work with you if you leave.

  • You own and control your domain, hosting, payment, email, analytics, and other provider accounts. We may help configure them, but they should be opened in your name with billing and recovery access you control.
  • You pay outside costs such as domain registration, hosting/provider charges, transaction fees, usage fees, and software or asset licenses unless the signed manifest expressly includes them.
  • Third-party material keeps its original license. Stock media, fonts, plugins, open-source code, platforms, and client-supplied assets do not become our property or yours beyond the rights their licenses allow.
  • Until final payment, finished files remain with us. After payment, we provide the agreed handoff and reasonable help moving the site; there is no exit fee for the paid deliverables.

Revisions, bugs & new scope

Every build includes the revision rounds stated in its signed manifest. One round is one consolidated list of feedback from the named decision maker, sent after the whole review group has agreed on it. Separate messages from several people may be returned for consolidation before a round begins.

  • A revision is a preference adjustment to work already included in the signed scope: for example, changing approved wording, replacing a supplied image, or adjusting an in-scope layout.
  • A bug or defect means the delivered work reproducibly fails to do what the signed manifest says it will do. Fixing that is not counted as a revision.
  • New scope includes extra pages, a new creative direction, added custom art or illustration, added motion, a new tool or integration, or a major restructure. It requires a written paid change order or a tier upgrade before work begins.
  • Additional revision rounds are also quoted and approved in writing before we perform them.

Monthly care & the proposed Hive

The non-binding website Care preview runs from Host at $25/mo through Partner at $349/mo. No plan is activated by this page or an inquiry. If Care is commercially accepted, it is month-to-month and its written scope must state the time cap, eligible work, activation, cancellation, handoff, and outside costs. The separate Hive automation line is not publicly operating.

  • The exact Care caps are catalog-controlled. Host is $25 with 0 edits and 0 minutes; Care Lite is $69 with up to 3 edits or 30 minutes; Care is $119 with up to 6 edits or 60 minutes; Care Plus is $199 with up to 10 edits or 120 minutes; Partner is $349 with up to 16 edits or 240 minutes. Where both limits appear, whichever is reached first controls.
  • Every Care task consumes the published cap. Communication, intake, research, editing, QA, reporting, calls, and deployment all count toward the stated monthly service time. The first reached edit-count or time limit controls where both are published.
  • Lower plans have no overflow. Host, Care Lite, Care, and Care Plus stop at their caps; the next plan is shown before more work is authorized. There are no silent overages.
  • Partner alone has a capped extra-hour path. One additional hour of otherwise-eligible Partner work may be approved in writing for $150 per website per paid month. It adds no extra call, review, refresh, page, feature, provider, or capability.
  • Hive setup fees, bundle discounts, and monthly figures on this site are planning estimates only. They do not create a purchase right, price guarantee, queue position, or service reservation.
  • If Care is activated and then canceled, it stops at the end of the current paid month after written confirmation. Hosting, backups, updates, and plan features stop; original paid deliverables and client-controlled accounts follow the written ownership and handoff terms.

Automation, messaging & AI boundaries

Any future Hive service would depend on the exact provider stack and workflow agreed for a project. Before any production launch, the written agreement would document enabled channels, providers, data flows, limits, and usage-based charges. A concept, mock-up, or advertised capability is not a promise that every carrier, number, integration, or third-party platform will support it.

  • A future customer would remain responsible for lawful contact. A production agreement would require the rights and consent needed for phone numbers, messages, review requests, follow-ups, and other customer data. The setup would include documented opt-out handling, while the customer would remain responsible for campaign content and contact lists.
  • No emergency or high-risk use. Any future Hive tools would not be emergency, medical, legal, financial, safety-critical, or human-resources decision systems. A production customer would need an ordinary contact path and human review for important messages.
  • Automations can fail or be delayed. Carriers and third-party services can filter, delay, reject, rate-limit, or change their products. Any future support scope must be written into the production agreement; uninterrupted delivery and business results will not be guaranteed.
  • AI output would need human judgment. Where a future workflow used AI, it could misunderstand, omit context, or produce an inaccurate response. A production setup would require narrow uses, escalation rules, approval of material customer-facing content, and human monitoring.
  • Privacy comes first. We minimize data and identify the production processors before launch. The Privacy Notice explains our own website and inquiry handling; a customer deployment may also need a project-specific data-processing schedule and your own customer-facing privacy notice.

Your content is your responsibility

Base footprint capacity assumes organized, client-supplied facts, words, and licensed assets. It includes layout and light editing only. The capped additional-content-shaping unit covers light drafting and editing from supplied, approved facts; interviews, independent research, claims substantiation, regulated copy, substantial net-new writing, and bulk reconstruction require custom written scope.

  • Only send images, logos, and text you own or have permission to use. If you’re not sure, tell us and we’ll help you find something you can use freely.
  • You’re responsible for the completeness and accuracy of facts you supply or approve — including hours, prices, services, locations, licenses, policies, and claims. We do not independently verify them unless the written scope expressly says so.
  • If a claim on your site needs to be true by law (licensing, certifications, guarantees), that’s on you to stand behind.

Schedule, feedback & launch

If commercial authority is later opened and a project is accepted, the manifest gives the target schedule. Work starts only when the commercially authorized signed manifest, required payment, and the content or access listed as starting requirements are in hand. Client changes, missing material, and provider delays move the schedule.

  • Please send each approval or grouped feedback round within five business days of the review request so the reserved production window can continue.
  • After ten business days without requested feedback, content, or approval, we may pause the project and rebook the remaining work into the next available opening. The original target date then moves.
  • We will flag a known studio-side delay promptly. Dates dependent on registrars, hosts, app stores, carriers, payment providers, or other third parties are targets rather than guarantees.
  • Nothing launches until the named decision maker approves it in writing and the final balance is paid.

Refunds & walking away

Life happens, and we’d rather part on good terms than hold anyone hostage.

  • Cancel before work begins and we return the initial payment. After work begins, the initial payment — including a Card or Card Plus up-front payment — is credited against reserved time and completed work.
  • If you cancel mid-build, we send a written closeout showing the work completed and the fees earned. Any unearned balance is returned; if earned fees exceed the initial payment, the difference is due. Transferable partial files are delivered after all earned fees are paid.
  • A current Care month is not partly refunded. Cancellation ends billing after that paid month and the hosting handoff described above applies.

14-day defect warranty

For 14 calendar days after launch or final delivery, report any reproducible defect that makes the delivered work fail the signed manifest. We will correct a confirmed covered defect without charging a revision round.

The warranty does not cover new requests, preference changes, content or code changed by the client or another provider, third-party outages or product changes, or unsupported devices and browsers. After the 14-day window, work is handled through an active Care plan or a written quote.

The honest bit

We deliver the work stated in the signed manifest, but we do not guarantee rankings, traffic, calls, leads, bookings, sales, revenue, profit, or third-party platform uptime. Those results depend on the market, the business, customers, search systems, carriers, and providers outside our control. We will not invent reviews, borrow someone else’s logos, or report results that did not happen.
